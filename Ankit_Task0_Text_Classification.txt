  Why TF-IDF?
  -----------
  TF-IDF down-weights common words and amplifies discriminative terms,
  making it highly effective for multi-class newsgroup classification where
  topic-specific vocabulary is the strongest signal.

  Why SVM (LinearSVC)?
  --------------------
  Linear SVMs find optimal separating hyperplanes in high-dimensional
  sparse feature spaces. They are known to outperform other linear models
  on text classification because they maximize the margin and are robust
  to the curse of dimensionality inherent in bag-of-words representations.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 4 – SUMMARY OF OBSERVATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  1. BEST:   TF-IDF + LinearSVC   → 93.84% accuracy
             TF-IDF normalises term importance; SVM handles high-dimensional
             sparse data exceptionally well.

  2. GOOD:   TF-IDF + LR, CV + SVM, TF-IDF + NB all exceed 88%.
             Sparse bag-of-words features pair better with linear models.

  3. DECENT: Word2Vec + SVM (~84%) and Word2Vec + LR (~83%) are competitive
             but lose per-document term specificity through mean-pooling.

  4. WEAKER: Doc2Vec under-performs Word2Vec here (80% vs 84% with SVM)
             because inferring test vectors requires additional epochs and
             is sensitive to model quality.

  5. WORST:  Decision Trees perform poorly across all feature sets.
             High-dimensional sparse input causes extreme over-fitting
             and poor generalisation in tree-based methods.

  6. Note on MultinomialNB with embeddings: NB with Word2Vec/Doc2Vec
             requires a non-negative shift which distorts the distribution,
             explaining the performance drop compared to sparse features.
